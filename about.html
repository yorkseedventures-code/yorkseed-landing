From a train ride
to a global venture network.

Yorkseed started as a spontaneous act of generosity — a spreadsheet shared on a train to help founders navigate SXSW. What followed was unexpected, unstoppable, and is still growing.

How we started
"It started with a train ride."

During a three-hour journey from New York City to Washington, DC, our founder Jessica Sophia Wong created what would become the viral SXSW 2023 "Unofficial Events" master list — a curated spreadsheet of badge-free events happening across Austin. What began as a spontaneous project quickly became one of the most shared grassroots resources of the week.

Armed with a printed QR code, she walked the streets of Austin inviting founders and investors to scan and access the list. Within hours, it spread across the ecosystem. The signal was immediate and overwhelming.

With just one hour of planning, she hosted an exclusive Founders × Investors SXSW Mixer. It sold out immediately, with attendees turned away at the door.

Upon returning to New York, she expanded the concept — beginning with an Earth Day Charity Brunch and a curated WhatsApp group connecting founders and investors. That proof of concept evolved into Yorkseed™: an exclusive global venture network uniting high-caliber founders, strategic investors, and ecosystem leaders across major innovation hubs.

Today, Yorkseed connects a curated global network of founders, investors, and capital allocators across 50+ hubs and the world's most important innovation gatherings. Membership is selective. Every application is reviewed. Every member is re-verified annually.

Access is intentional. Connections are curated. Relationships compound.

The founder
Yorkseed is not an event company. It is a venture platform built on trust, access, and long-term relationship capital.
Jessica Sophia Wong · Founder & CEO · Yorkseed

Jessica Sophia Wong built Yorkseed from a spreadsheet and a belief: that the right connection at the right moment changes everything — and that too many serious founders and investors are locked out of those moments not by ability, but by access.

In venture capital, credibility often comes from pedigrees — MBAs, investment banking, big tech. Jessica had none of that. What she had was a QR code, a gift for connecting people, and a willingness to walk the streets of Austin and invite strangers to scan a link.

That instinct — to open rooms rather than gatekeep them — is the foundation of everything Yorkseed is and is becoming.
